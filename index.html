Snap Design Academy Product Design

ROLE: VISUAL DESIGNER AND DESIGN SCHOLAR | 2021
DURATION: APPROX. 2-3 MONTHS

During the Summer of 2021 I had the incredible oppurtunity to participate in the 2021 Snap Design Academy hosted by Snap Inc. and LA-Tech.org. My participation within the program was an immense privilege, as I was able to have access to: fireside chats, design workshops, individualized mentorship, support systems, and technology.
Much of my learning were performed when projects required cross-discinplinary teams that enabled me to utilize as much data as I needed to create solutions that addressed complex problems. Some works required the analysis and utilization of large amounts of crowd-sourced data while other projects required intentional communication to create product or services that were free of personal biases. Our projects allowed me to exercise my skills in project management, product research and analysis, and telecommunicative teamwork — all which were new ventures for me. By the end of the program, I was confident in my ability to create product and services that utilize the immense amount of networks available whether its data, people, or experiences.
Below I will showcase my team's Capstone project and presentation that we presented to a Judge Panel and CEO of Snap Inc (2021), Evan Speigal. Our product is a Snapchat AR Lens that addresses Environmental Sustainability within Southern California's most dense concrete jungles.

Project: DreamGreen Lens in Partnership with Los Angeles Neighborhood Land Trust and Snap Inc.

Responsibility(s): Creative Direction, Visual Design, and UI/UX Design

Objective: Create a product or service addressing Environmental Sustainability in cooperation with Los Angeles Neighborhood Land Trust utilizing technology(s) provided by Snap Inc. and Snapchat.

Sole Folks Visual Identity

Project: Transdiscinplinary Partnership with Sole Folks | Leimert Park
ROLE: VISUAL DESIGNER
DURATION: APPROX. 14 WEEKS
My Deliverables: Brand Guidelines (Print + Digital), Branded Stationary, Visual Identity Design Refinement, Brand Elements (Pattern / Monogram), Retail Signage, and White-label Product Design

While attaining my Bachelor's at ArtCenter College of Design I had the immense oppurtunity to partner with Sole Folks at Leimert Park, a Southern California retail shop and educational platform located within the heart of Los Angeles. Some course goals included providing a commercial-level redesign, brand and visual identity refinements, as well as print materials for fundraising and marketing assets for Sole Folks at Leimert Park. I gained immense knowledge of logistoical systems within retail businesses and how design thinking play an influential role in retail development. The course resulted in my concepts and designs being implemented as well as receive syndication via media outlets like CBS News Los Angeles | KCAL.
